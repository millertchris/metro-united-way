COVID-19 UPDATES From Metro United Way

Metro United Way takes very seriously the potential spread of the COVID-19 virus and feels our entire community must play a role in helping contain the virus. In following recommendations from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the Governor’s office, the Kentucky Department of Health, as well as the Mayor’s office and Louisville Metro Department of Public Health & Wellness, we have closed our offices and directing all Metro United Way (MUW) employees to telework through April, 2021.

Our community must Stand United in the face of this COVID-19 situation to ensure an expedient resolution.  MUW leadership will continually assess the ongoing COVID-19 updates and remain in constant contact with health and government officials to inform when we will resume normal operations.

We have also ensured our 2-1-1 resource may be leveraged as a source of information and referral to the community. If you or someone you know needs help, CALL 2-1-1 or TEXT your zip code to 898211. Our call specialists are available 24/7 and will help connect you to food, housing, health, education and other resources available in our community.

    Pandemic-EBT (P-EBT)

    The Pandemic-EBT (P-EBT) is now available to Kentucky households with a child or children receiving for free/reduced-priced meals at school. The P-EBT provides a benefits card families can use to buy food to replace free school breakfast and lunch while schools are closed. Program enrollment/access will differ based on the below household circumstances.

    • Group I—Benefits will be automatically loaded on existing EBT cards for children already receiving SNAP benefits.
    • Group II—Benefits will be loaded on new EBT cards and automatically mailed to families of students who access programs—including Medicaid, SNAP, and TANF, as well as youth in foster care—but who do not currently have an EBT card.
    • Group III—Eligible households—those with students who qualify for free/reduced-price school meals AND students who attend schools that provide free meals for all students—with children not currently enrolled in these programs must apply for P-EBT benefits at benefind.ky.gov . Applications will be taken from June 1 through June 30, 2020. The application will require student name, address, date of birth, and Statewide Student Identification (SSID). After applying, eligible families will receive a new EBT card in the mail with their benefit.
    • More information can be found on the Kentucky Pandemic Electronic Benefit Transfer Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ).

    Louisville Water Company Suspending Shut-Offs for Customers
    The Louisville Water company is suspending water shut-offs for failure to pay until further notice, an official with the utility confirmed Friday.

    About 2,000 people have their water service shut-off each month, but as the COVID-19 pandemic spreads and officials encourage residents to maintain good hygiene the city’s water company will cease all shut-offs for residents who cannot afford to pay their bill, said Vince Guenthner, a senior utilities consultant with Louisville Water.

    Residents do not need to take any action to ensure they won’t have their water shut-off; Guenthner said the utility simply is not executing the shut-offs.

  • Opening a bank account

    • Encourages Louisville-area residents to open a Bank On certified account where holders can access mobile banking, direct deposit, bill pay, ATM’s and more through area banks and credit unions with which they are familiar.
    • Check the listing of Bank On Louisville financial institution partners at bankonlouisville.org. Bank and credit union partners of Bank On Louisville offer accounts that are safe, affordable and functional. Several partners also offer Bank On Certified accounts which meet a rigorous set of standards related to affordability, consumer safety and functionality.  Specifically, these certified accounts do not allow overdraft or non-sufficient funds fees, so holders will never be hit with a surprise fee. They also charge $5 or less per month and do not charge for mobile banking, deposits, withdrawals, or bill payments.
    • The process to open an account varies by financial institution, but many offer the ability to open an account online. In general, accounts can be opened online, in-person, over the phone or via a mobile app. A full list of bank and credit union partners and those with certified accounts can be found at: www.bankonlouisville.org.
    • Not having a banking account on file with the IRS means that your economic impact payment will take longer to receive because it will come via paper check in the mail.
    • Having a banking account will also ensure that other forms of income or benefits are received timely and safely – unemployment insurance, paychecks, tax refunds, etc.

    UI Employer Handbook:

    The Kentucky Education and Workforce Development Cabinet has developed a UI Employer Handbook for COVID-19. Please refer to pages 12 and 13 of this document for guidance on helping employees transition from unemployment back to employment.

    • Unemployment Insurance Employer Handbook for COVID-19

    Key things to know:

    • Notify employees as soon as possible of the date they are expected to return to work.
    • According to the UI Employer Handbook, furloughed or laid-off employees may continue to draw unemployment benefits until their employer “begins operations.” If an employee has already filed a claim for this time-period, they should contact Kentucky’s UI help line to adjust this information: 502-564-2900
    • Please refer to page 13 of the UI Employer Handbook for guidance on what to do if a furloughed or laid-off employees chooses not to return to work after your business has restarted operations or requested that they return to work.
    • As an employer, you are entitled to have an opportunity to protest any claim for unemployment benefits by an employee. Learn more at the Kentucky Career Center’s Benefit Appeals website.

    Trends across the country have indicated that employers are having difficulty in filling openings and recalling workers. GLI has partnered with KentuckianaWorks to launch a Job Seeker Connection program.
